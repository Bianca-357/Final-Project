INGREDIENTS FOR JOLLOF RICE

  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeds removed
  • 3-4 medium tomatoes, quartered (or 1 can of diced/crushed tomatoes)
  • 3-4 cloves garlic
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet or habanero peppers (adjust to taste)
  • ½ cup water
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups jasmine or basmati rice, thoroughly rinsed until water runs clear
  • 2-3 cups chicken, beef, or vegetable stock (or water)
  • 2 chicken bouillon cubes, crushed
  • 1 Tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Mixed vegetables like carrots and peas, or a protein of choice (chicken, beef, etc.)

INGREDIENTS FOR FRIED RICE

  • Eggs

  • Carrots
  • Green pepper
  • Spring onions
  • Seasoning tablet (optional)

WAAKYE INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup black-eyed peas
  • 2 cups white rice (jasmine or basmati recommended)
  • 4-5 dried sorghum leaves (also known as waakye leaves or millet leaves; essential for color)
  • 5-7 cups water (approximately)
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda (optional, enhances the color)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons coconut oil (optional, for flavor and texture)

BANKU INGREDIENTS

  • Corn dough: A fermented dough made from cornmeal.
  • Cassava dough: A fermented dough made from cassava root.
  • Salt: Added to taste.
  • Water: Used for mixing and cooking.
  • Dough ratio: Traditional recipes often use a mix of fermented corn and cassava dough. A common ratio is two parts corn dough to one part cassava dough, but this can be adjusted based on preference and desired texture
  • Dough preparation: The corn and cassava are first fermented separately to create the doughs. The doughs are then mixed together with water and salt to begin the cooking process.

FUFU INGREDIENTS

  • Cassava: Also known as yuca, it is the most common base for fufu. It can be used fresh or fermented.
  • Plantain: Usually unripe or green plantains are used, which are mixed with the cassava to create a smoother texture.
  • Alternative ingredients
  • Yam: Pounded yam fufu is popular, and the ingredients are pounded until smooth.
  • Cocoyam: Can be used along with or instead of cassava and plantain.
  • Flour: In modern or less traditional versions, flours made from cassava, maize, or semolina can be used as a substitute for the fresh roots.

  • Gari: Fermented cassava meal, which can be sprinkled on top or mixed in.

  • Other seasonings: Such as curry powder, bouillon cubes, or paprika.
  • Plantains: Often served on the side, typically fried plantains are used.

OKRO SOUP INGREDIENTS

  • Okra: The primary ingredient, providing a unique texture. 
  • Protein: Common additions include meat (like beef or goat), fish, or shrimp.
  • Palm oil: Used as a cooking base and for flavor.
  • Spices and seasonings: This can include onions, tomatoes, peppers, and various spices like crayfish, locust beans, and stock cubes.
  • Vegetables: Other vegetables like spinach, ugu leaves, or ewedu can be added.

PALM NUT SOUP INGREDIENTS

  • Palm nut pulp: The base of the soup, extracted from the palm nut fruit.
  • Protein: A variety of meats (beef, goat, chicken), fish (smoked or fresh), and sometimes seafood like crabs are used.
  • Aromatics: Onions, garlic, and ginger form a flavorful base.
  • Spices and seasonings: Ingredients like tomatoes, habanero peppers, and various spices are added for flavor and heat.
  • Leafy greens: Spinach or bitter leaf is often added for nutrition and a slightly bitter taste.

GROUNDNUT SOUP INGREDIENTS

  • Peanuts/Peanut Butter: The essential base, providing flavor and thickness.
  • Protein: Assorted meats, chicken, or fish are common additions.
  • Vegetables & Aromatics: Onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and peppers (e.g., habanero or scotch bonnet) form the aromatic base.
  • Broth: Meat or vegetable stock is used to achieve the desired consistency.
  • Seasoning: Salt, bouillon powder, paprika, and local spices enhance the flavor.

OMOTUO INGREDIENTS

  • Rice:Long-grain white rice, such as Jasmine rice, is a common choice.
  • Water
  • Salt

KONTOMIRE STEW INGREDIENTS

  • Kontomire leaves: The main ingredient (spinach is a common substitute).
  • Palm oil: Provides the traditional flavor and color.
  • Protein: Various options are used, such as smoked mackerel, salted fish (koobi or momoni), cow skin (willie), or eggs.
  • Agushi (melon seeds): Ground into a paste or powder to add a nutty flavor and thicken the stew.
  • Aromatics: A blend of fresh tomatoes, onions, ginger, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers (kpakposhito).
  • Seasoning: Salt, all-purpose seasoning, and sometimes ingredients like dried shrimp are used.

FRIED YAM

  • Yam: A tuber yam is the primary ingredient.
  • Vegetable oil: Needed for deep-frying the yam.
  • Salt: To season the yam.
  • Water: Used to parboil the yam before frying.
  • Seasonings: Onion powder, garlic powder, pepper, turmeric, and thyme are frequently used.
  • Spices: Curry powder, chili powder, and paprika are popular choices.
  • For sauces: Ingredients like tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions are often blended for a side sauce.

MEAT PIE INGREDIENTS

  • Meat: Ground beef and pork are common choices.
  • Vegetables: Onions, carrots, and potatoes are frequently used.
  • Spices: Cumin, garlic, thyme, sage, and various curry or Cajun blends provide flavor.
  • Thickening agents: Flour, cornstarch, or mashed potatoes are often added to ensure the filling coheres into a gravy-like consistency and does not make the crust soggy.

FISH PIE INGREDIENTS

  • 1 can (6 oz or 3 oz cans, depending on desired richness) of boneless, skinless salmon or tuna, well-drained
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (scallions)
  • 1/4 cup sour cream or cream cheese, softened
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried dill weed or 1 Tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Pastry & Finish:
  • 1 sheet of frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten (for egg wash)
  • Optional: Everything But the Bagel seasoning, or a small slit on top for venting

BOFROT INGREDIENTS

  • All-purpose flour
  • Sugar
  • Yeast
  • Lukewarm water or milk
  • Salt
  • Nutmeg (optional, but a common addition for authentic flavor)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)
  • Optional ingredients like evaporated milk, vanilla essence, or butter can also be incorporated to enhance the flavor and texture.
